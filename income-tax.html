Frequently Asked Questions

What’s the difference between Old and New Tax Regimes in FY 2025-26?

The Old Regime allows deductions like Section 80C (₹1.5L), 80D, and HRA but has higher tax rates for lower incomes. The New Regime has lower tax rates and higher slabs (e.g., 0% up to ₹3L, 5% up to ₹7L) but only allows standard deduction (₹50,000) and Section 80CCD(2).

How is HRA exemption calculated?

HRA exemption is the minimum of: (1) HRA received, (2) Rent paid minus 10% of basic salary, (3) 50% of basic salary (metro cities) or 40% (non-metro).

What are the tax slabs for FY 2025-26?

Assuming FY 2024-25 slabs apply: Old Regime (Below 60): 0% (₹0–₹2.5L), 5% (₹2.5L–₹5L), 20% (₹5L–₹10L), 30% (>₹10L). New Regime: 0% (₹0–₹3L), 5% (₹3L–₹7L), 10% (₹7L–₹10L), 15% (₹10L–₹12L), 20% (₹12L–₹15L), 30% (>₹15L).

Are capital gains taxed differently in FY 2025-26?

Yes, equity-based LTCG is taxed at 10% (above ₹1.25L exemption), and STCG at 15%. These rates are applied separately from income tax slabs.
