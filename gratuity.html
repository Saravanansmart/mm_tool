Frequently Asked Questions (Updated 2025 Rules)

Who is eligible for gratuity now?

Permanent: 5 years (or 4 years + 240 days)
Contract/Fixed-Term: Only 1 year of service

What changed in wage definition?

Now includes Basic + DA + Retaining Allowance and must be ≥50% of total CTC — increasing payout for many employees.

What is the new tax-free limit?

₹25,00,000 for private sector (increased in Budget 2025 to match Central Govt).

How are fractional years calculated?

6+ months in final year = full year rounded up
e.g. 10 years 8 months → counted as 11 years

Can gratuity be forfeited?

Only in case of termination due to moral turpitude or willful damage. Otherwise, full amount is payable.

What if I resign before 5 years?

Permanent employees: No gratuity unless 4 years + 240 days completed.
Contract employees: Eligible after 1 year.

Is gratuity paid on resignation?

Yes, if eligibility is met. Employer must pay within 30 days of it becoming due, else 10% interest applies.

How to claim gratuity if employer refuses?

Approach the Controlling Authority under Payment of Gratuity Act within 90 days. Keep payslips, appointment letter, and service proof.
