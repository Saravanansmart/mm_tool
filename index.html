What are the key GST 2.0 changes effective 22 September 2025?

GST 2.0 introduced slab rationalisation, several rate reductions for essentials and clarified transitional treatments around Input Tax Credit (ITC). Businesses should update product/service tax mapping and reconcile ITC balances as per official FAQs. For supply-specific details, review the GST notifications and council FAQs linked in our GST article.

How should businesses treat existing Input Tax Credit (ITC) around the GST rate changes?

Transitional guidance generally allows ITC availed up to the cut-off date to remain, but you must reconcile your ledgers and supplier invoices. For supplies where concessional rates apply only if ITC is not claimed, apply the concession only for future supplies where ITC wasn't availed. Speak with your tax consultant if you have complex cross-period supplies.

Do we need to update MRPs, POS and e-commerce listings after GST 2.0?

Yes. Update POS templates and e-commerce listings with new tax-inclusive prices where rates changed. Clear price communication avoids customer confusion and potential compliance flags from regulators.
