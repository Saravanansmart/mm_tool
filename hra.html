HRA Exemption Rule Breakdown (The "Minimum of Three" Rule)

The maximum HRA amount exempt from tax is the least of the following three conditions (per monthly basis):

  • HRA Received: The actual amount of House Rent Allowance received from your employer.
  • Rent Paid - 10% of Salary: Actual annual rent paid minus 10% of your Basic Salary + DA.
  • Statutory Percentage:
    • 50% of (Basic Salary + DA) if living in a Metro City (Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai).
    • 40% of (Basic Salary + DA) if living in a Non-Metro City.

What is an HRA Exemption Calculator?

An HRA Exemption Calculator is an online tool that estimates the tax-exempt portion of House Rent Allowance (HRA) in India based on basic salary, HRA received, rent paid, and whether you live in a metro or non-metro city.

How is HRA exemption calculated in India?

HRA exemption is determined by taking the minimum of three calculated values (HRA received, Rent Paid minus 10% of salary, and the statutory percentage of salary‚Äî50% for metro, 40% for non-metro). This is the amount exempt from tax.

Which cities are considered metro cities for HRA exemption?

For HRA exemption, the official metro cities in India are Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, and Chennai (DMKC). Residing in one of these cities qualifies you for the 50% basic salary exemption limit.

Is HRA exemption applicable under the New Tax Regime (FY 2025-26)?

No, HRA exemption under Section 10(13A) is generally not available if you opt for the simplified New Tax Regime (Section 115BAC) in FY 2025-26. This calculator assumes calculation under the Old Tax Regime for individuals aiming to maximize tax benefits.
